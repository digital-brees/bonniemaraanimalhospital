Diagnostic Medicine

Diagnostics & imaging.

Answers, faster

From wellness screening blood tests to advanced imaging, our diagnostics give us fast, accurate answers—so we can care for your pet without delay.

Lab testing

In our laboratory.

  1. 01

    In-house laboratory

    In-house testing lets our team diagnose many illnesses while you wait. Our capabilities include essential blood and urine testing, parasite testing, dermatology testing, and more.

    Bedside results give us critical health information in minutes—so we can formulate and begin a treatment plan without the lag of sending tests out.

  2. 02

    Reference laboratory

    For specialized and non-urgent tests, we partner with top-tier reference laboratories. They provide comprehensive services—including blood panels, histopathology, and cultures—to help us reach an accurate diagnosis.

    Reference labs complement our in-house testing by giving us a more complete picture of your pet’s health.

Imaging

Diagnostic imaging.

01

Digital X-ray

X-rays are a vital tool for examining your pet’s bones, joints, chest, abdomen, bladder, and other internal structures. Our technology delivers fast, high-quality images while minimizing radiation exposure, and our veterinarians may consult a radiology specialist for expert review.

Reasons we may recommend an X-ray

  • Suspected fracture or soft tissue injury
  • Vomiting
  • Urinary tract problems
  • Abnormal abdominal palpation
  • Heart murmur
  • Coughing or difficulty breathing
  • Urinary stone analysis
02

Ultrasound

A non-invasive technique that provides detailed, real-time images of your pet’s internal organs without radiation. We use ultrasound to evaluate organs, obtain biopsies, collect urine, and monitor hospitalized patients in a cost-effective way.

Abdominal ultrasound

A complete scan of the liver, kidneys, spleen, bladder, adrenal glands, pancreas, gallbladder, and intestines—essential for diagnosing tumors, evaluating organs, and guiding needle biopsies.

Echocardiography

Cardiac ultrasound that evaluates the heart’s structure and function—used to diagnose and monitor heart conditions and to confirm safe heart function before anesthesia.
