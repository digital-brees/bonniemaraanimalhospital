Dental Health

Dentistry & oral care.

In brief

Pet dental health is often overlooked—but it has critical implications for your pet’s quality of life and well-being.

Oral health

Why dental care matters.

Did you know that pets need the same oral care as people, including daily toothbrushing and routine professional cleanings? Unfortunately, many pets suffer from poor oral health, which can lead to pain, infection, and systemic problems affecting the heart, liver, and kidneys.

Proper dental care helps prevent plaque and tartar buildup, keeps your pet’s teeth and gums healthy, and contributes to a good quality of life. Our team provides professional dentistry services to prevent dental problems and treat existing conditions—and we’ll help you keep up the routine at home, too.

By age 3nearly all pets are affected by periodontal disease

The hidden threat

Periodontal disease.

It begins when sticky plaque coats the teeth and hardens into tartar, allowing bacteria to accumulate and inflame the gums. Left untreated, it progresses and causes pain, tooth loss, and difficulty eating or playing. Bacteria from infected gum tissue can even leak into the bloodstream and affect important organs, including the heart.

Our approach

What a dental visit involves.

1

Dental examination

Our veterinarians thoroughly inspect your pet’s teeth and gums for plaque, tartar, gum disease, broken or loose teeth, and other oral problems. A brief dental exam happens at every routine wellness visit.

2

Dental X-rays

Most tooth problems occur below the gum line, where you can’t see them. Dental X-rays reveal each tooth’s entire structure, including the root, to catch abscesses, bone loss, tooth resorption, and unerupted teeth.

3

Dental cleaning

Trained team members remove plaque and tartar above and below the gum line, take X-rays, and polish your pet’s teeth. Our veterinarians perform a comprehensive oral exam and treat problem teeth—gently and thoroughly.

4

Dental surgery

When teeth are diseased, surgical care may be needed. We perform a range of procedures—including oral tumor removal—using nerve blocks and injectable pain medication to keep your pet comfortable throughout their stay.

Your pet’s safety

Safe anesthesia, always.

Anesthesia is essential for dental procedures, keeping your pet still and pain-free during treatment. While anesthesia-free dentistry seems appealing, it can cause extreme stress and doesn’t allow for a thorough cleaning, X-rays, or necessary treatments.

We take every precaution to ensure safe anesthesia — including:

  • A thorough pre-anesthetic evaluation
  • Close monitoring throughout the procedure

Between visits

Care that continues at home.

At-home toothbrushing is the most effective way to maintain dental health between professional cleanings. Brushing daily with a pet-safe toothpaste and soft-bristled brush removes plaque, reduces harmful bacteria, and keeps your pet’s breath fresh.

Our team can

  • Demonstrate brushing
  • Share tips for helping pets acclimate
  • Recommend safe, effective products — including options for pets who won’t accept brushing
