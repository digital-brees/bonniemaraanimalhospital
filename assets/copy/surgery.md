## Intro
We understand that surgery can cause anxiety for pet owners, which is why we prioritize patient safety and comfort. Our skilled veterinary surgeons can perform a range of routine to complex procedures, and our support team is dedicated to providing compassionate and expert care. From check-in to pick-up, you can rest assured your furry pal is in good hands.

## Spay and neuter
Spaying and neutering are essential procedures for preventing unwanted pregnancies and promoting long-term pet health. While spaying and neutering are elective procedures, we recommend that all pet owners strongly consider them to reduce the risks of reproductive cancers and undesirable hormone-driven behaviors. The timing for a spay or neuter procedure depends on your pet's gender, species, age, and expected adult size—ask our veterinary team for details.

## Laparoscopic spay
A laparoscopic spay offers a minimally invasive alternative to traditional spay surgery. This advanced technique uses small incisions, a camera, and specialized instruments to remove a female pet's ovaries but leave the uterus behind. The benefits of laparoscopic spay surgery include faster healing, reduced pain, and fewer complications. We are proud to offer this cutting-edge option to minimize surgical trauma and provide a better surgery experience for your furry pal.

## Soft tissue surgery
Our veterinary surgeons can perform a wide range of soft tissue surgeries, which are procedures performed on the skin, muscles, or organs. Surgeries in our facility range in complexity from minor mass removals to emergency abdominal procedures. Our hospital uses modern surgical techniques, advanced monitoring equipment, and qualified team members to ensure pet safety and comfort at all times.

## Orthopedic surgery
Our hospital is proud to offer specialized orthopedic procedures to reduce pain and restore function in pets with orthopedic injuries or diseases. Our surgeons have advanced training and extensive experience performing complex orthopedic procedures, reducing complications, and developing comprehensive post-operative rehabilitation programs. Our advanced surgical facilities, equipment, and training allow us to provide exceptional orthopedic care in a primary care setting.

## TPLO
Tibial plateau leveling osteotomy (TPLO) is an advanced orthopedic procedure used to treat cranial cruciate ligament (CCL) injuries in dogs and restore normal knee function. Our surgical team has extensive experience performing TPLO surgeries and ensuring optimal post-operative recovery. TPLO is a good choice for dogs of any size or activity level but provides especially good outcomes for large, active breeds.

## Non-invasive surgery (Endoscopy)
Endoscopy is a non-invasive diagnostic tool that allows us to examine internal pet structures without a surgical incision. An endoscope is a rigid or flexible tube equipped with a fiber optic camera and tiny instruments to visualize and manipulate tissues. Although endoscopy requires general anesthesia, this approach can drastically reduce recovery times and complication rates over traditional surgery.

## Laser surgery
Our hospital is equipped with a surgical laser, which we can use in place of a traditional scalpel blade to minimize bleeding and reduce post-operative discomfort. The laser can cut and coagulate tissues simultaneously, which gives our surgeons a better view and can reduce anesthetic time and complication rates.
