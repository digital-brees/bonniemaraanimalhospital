Surgical Care

Surgical care.

Our approach

We understand that surgery can cause anxiety for pet owners, which is why we prioritize patient safety and comfort.

Routine procedures

Core surgical care.

  1. 01

    Spay & neuter

    Spaying and neutering are essential procedures for preventing unwanted pregnancies and promoting long-term pet health. While elective, we recommend all pet owners strongly consider them to reduce the risks of reproductive cancers and undesirable hormone-driven behaviors. The right timing depends on your pet’s gender, species, age, and expected adult size—ask our veterinary team for details.

  2. 02

    Soft tissue surgery

    Our veterinary surgeons perform a wide range of soft tissue surgeries—procedures on the skin, muscles, or organs—ranging in complexity from minor mass removals to emergency abdominal procedures. We use modern surgical techniques, advanced monitoring equipment, and qualified team members to ensure pet safety and comfort at all times.

From check‑in to pick‑up. You can rest assured your furry pal is in good hands.

Beyond the basics

Advanced & minimally invasive.

Our team is equipped for specialized and minimally invasive procedures, performed with the same focus on patient safety and comfort—often meaning smaller incisions, less trauma, and a smoother recovery for your furry pal.

  1. 03

    Laparoscopic spay

    A minimally invasive alternative to traditional spay surgery. This advanced technique uses small incisions, a camera, and specialized instruments—offering faster healing, reduced pain, and fewer complications.

  2. 04

    Orthopedic surgery

    Specialized procedures to reduce pain and restore function in pets with orthopedic injuries or diseases. Advanced surgical facilities, equipment, and training let us provide exceptional orthopedic care in a primary care setting.

  3. 05

    TPLO

    Tibial plateau leveling osteotomy treats cranial cruciate ligament (CCL) injuries in dogs and restores normal knee function. A good choice for dogs of any size, with especially strong outcomes for large, active breeds.

  4. 06

    Endoscopy

    A non-invasive tool that lets us examine internal structures without a surgical incision, using a camera-equipped scope. Though it requires general anesthesia, this approach can drastically reduce recovery times and complication rates.

  5. 07

    Laser surgery

    Our surgical laser can replace a traditional scalpel to minimize bleeding and reduce post-operative discomfort. By cutting and coagulating tissue at once, it gives our surgeons a better view and can reduce anesthetic time.
